THE GPS BLIND SPOT

Five hours and forty
minutes you can't
account for.

That's the gap between "arrived" and "left" on your tracker. Inside it, a vehicle sits through fuel, intake, cleaning and dispatch prep, and none of it is written down anywhere your ops manager can see. When a client asks why a vehicle took two days to turn around, the honest answer is usually "we're not sure."

DepotTrack puts a checkpoint inside that gap, so the story between the gate and the road is finally on record.

THE CHECKPOINT PIPELINE

One QR scan.
Six checkpoints. Zero guesswork.

Every vehicle moves through the same six gates on the way from entry to dispatch. A scan at each one, a few seconds with a phone camera, writes the depot's story as it happens, not from memory at the end of the week.

01
EntryVehicle logged the moment it crosses the gate: reg, driver, time.
02
FuelFuel level captured before the vehicle moves any further into the yard.
03
Depot intakeOdometer, condition notes and damage flagged on arrival, not disputed later.
04
CleaningWash and valet status tracked so nothing dispatches half-done.
05
Dispatch readySigned off as fit to go, the last check before it's back on the road.
06
Dispatch outExit scan closes the loop and starts the dwell-time clock over.

LIVE VISIBILITY

One board shows every vehicle on site and exactly which checkpoint it's sitting at, right now. No phone calls to the yard.

DWELL TIME

Dwell time, automatically

The clock starts at entry and splits by checkpoint, so you can see exactly where a vehicle lost time, and who was responsible.

COMPLIANCE

Alerts before it's a problem

Vehicles that overstay a threshold, miss a checkpoint, or come due for service get flagged before a client or auditor finds out first.

AUDIT TRAIL

MULTI-STATION
